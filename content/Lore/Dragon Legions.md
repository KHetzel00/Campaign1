Each Champion of Bahamut/Sardior takes with them a whole legion of soldiers that are constantly with them.

Like much of the other church customs no one is forced to be in a legion, instead they seek it out due to the glory to be held in such a position.

The Legions do break the mold however, allowing non-clergy members among their ranks. Be they men seeking glory or simply followers of Bahamut who are not dragonborn, even allowing non clergy to lead individual groups of the Legion

## Legion Makeup

Legions number in size of ~20,000 soldiers. (Leadership and the honor guard are not counted in this number)

10 Soldiers (2-8 Paladins, 2 Clerics, 0-6 Axillary soldiers) = Squad (Leader, Second in Command)
10 Squads = Company(Leader, Second in Command, Standard Bearer)
10 Companies = Division(Leader, Second in Command, Standard Bearer)
10 Divisions = Army (Leader, Second in Command, Standard Bearer) 
2 Armies = Legion (Champion, Second in Command, Standard Bearer, +100 of the Champions greatest warriors which fight directly alongside them)

Each Legion is marked by a number from 1-5, though these numbers do not correlate to importance, simply the order in which they were founded

##### Gaining Station

Stations are decided through merit and steel. There is no promotion simply for being around a long time or even just having combat experience. One must prove themselves in having the ability to lead a squad.

These are usually done with wargames, in which a squad member will challenge their squad leader to see who is the better strategist. 

In these matches the Champion grants the two combatants any 9 soldiers of their choosing from his honor guard and then lead them into a mock battle. The victor becomes the squad leader.

In other fighting forces one would think this could lead to bad blood but it actually leads to the opposite within the Legion, both parties simply use the battle as a learning experience and still hold great respect for each other as the true honor is being a member of the Legion at all.

For stations higher than Sergeant(Squad Leader) there is a tournament held every 5 years in which anyone who wants to lead a larger grouping must perform the same wargames mentioned above against every other leader of their current station who wants to.

EXAMPLE: 
Say 5 of the 10 Sergeants in a Company want to rank up to Captain. Those 5, plus the current Captain and Lieutenant must compete to determine the new Captain and Lieutenant. The other 5 Sergeants do not have to participate.
Similarly if none of the Sergeants want to rank up, and the Lieutenant is happy not being a Captain then no tournament has to happen.

This is how every rank from Lieutenant to General is determined

##### Important Stations

- Each Squad is lead by a Sergeant who is allowed to pick a second in command of their choosing, commonly called the Corporal
- Each Company Is lead by a Captain whose second in command is whoever got second place in the tournament, called the Lieutenant
- Each Division is lead by a Colonel whose second in command is whoever got second place in the tournament, called the Lieutenant Colonel
- Each Army is lead by a General whose second in command is whoever got second place in the tournament, called the Lieutenant General
- The Legion is lead by the Champion, whose second in command is their successor who has been picked by the Order of Platinum/Ruby, called the Arch-Initiate

##### Legion naming structure

Everything, from Squads to the Legion are named with numbers, all pertaining to the order in which they were created, superseding Legion lines. 
i.e. Armies 1 & 2 are in Legion 1 and Armies 3 & 4 are in Legion 2. Similarly Legion 1 contains Squad 1 ("First Squad") and Legion 5 contains Squad 10,000 ("Ten-Thousandth Squad")

These numbers are the groups official name for logistical purposes but many squads, companies, divisions, and even armies have self proclaimed names and nick names.

## A Further Breakdown of Roles

In any Army, there is always one logistic division and one siege division.
Both of these divisions are field combat ready but are prepared to serve a secondary purpose

The Logistic Division does just as its name entails. All logistics for the Army from food to weapons and armor are acquired and distributed by this company to the others (and itself)

The Siege Division also has a very apt name. While all of its soldiers are proficient and can fight in melee, they specialize in siege warfare both offensively and defensively. Constructing grand trebuchets and siege towers, but also creating siege camps and trenchworks.

Below that each Company will designate 1 Squad as its Logistic Squad, taking resources from the Logistic Division and giving them out to the company

The Logistic Divisions do go into field combat, with the exception being during sieges. During a siege, be it attacking or defending, the task of providing food, armor, and munitions is more important. However they will be used as a last resort.

##### Weaponry

All squads are proficient in both melee and ranged combat and my be called upon for either depending on what the situation needs.

Each soldier in the army is called upon to use whichever melee weapon they most prefer, however when it comes to ranged combat there are some differences.

Since a given situation may call for any type of ranged weapon companies are assigned certain ones they are expected to master:
* 4 Companies of each Division are expected to master longbows
* 3 Companies of each Division are expected to master muskets
* 3 Companies of each Division are expected to master heavy crossbows
While every company can be used in ranged combat usually only 2-3 per division will be used for ranged all at once (usually 1 longbow with 1-2 of either musket or crossbow)

Longbows: Called upon in almost every situation, often supplementing the other two
Muskets: Called upon when in open field or in siege defense
Heavy Crossbow: Called upon when in an offensive siege as they are more reliable to hit from a longer distance than muskets

# Origins

Dragons are almost as old as Oerth itself. Their progenitor, Io, created Oerth from the void.  
  
Shortly after the creation of Oerth Io sought to fill his new empty world, so he created for himself three children: His daughter Tiamat and his sons Bahamut and Sardior. 


He created each of them so that they would populate Oerth with dragonkind. However Io, a truly neutral being, could not foresee what separating his essence into children would do.


Bahamut, with his beautiful platinum scales, saw himself as an arbiter of good in the world. He created the metallic dragons: Brass, Bronze, Copper, Gold, and Silver. He instilled in them the ability to do good for the lesser races that began appearing on Oerth.
  

Sardior, with his brilliant shining ruby scales, felt he had no duties to the planet, nor anyone for that matter. He created the gem dragons: Amethyst, Crystal, Emerald, Sapphire, and Topaz. He instilled upon them a curiosity for the world, and sent them out to learn all that they could.


Finally, Tiamat, with her 5 chromatic heads, hated the planet, she didn’t see the point in wasting her time with it, but in a bid to get her fathers approval she went ahead with his orders. She created the 5 chromatic dragon types: Black, Blue, Green, White, and Red, and told them to do good, much like her brother Bahamut’s children.
  

Io created Sardior first, but after seeing his apathy towards Oerth Io decided to create a pair of twins with hopes that they would work together to help his world. He couldn’t have been more wrong. The twins had a rivalry since birth, always one upping each other to gain their fathers approval.


Tiamat frequently employed tricks to make Bahamut look bad to their father while making herself look good, however Io always seemed to favor his platinum child to her.


This is when Tiamat got an idea, an idea so drastic that it would forever change the course of history for Oerth.


One night while her father and brothers were sleeping, she plucked a few of Bahamut’s scales and snuck into Sardior’s chambers. It was there she, in cold blood, murdered her sleeping brother. Sardior didn’t know or understand what was happening until he saw his blood pool by his head. In his last moments he looked up to his sister, covered in his blood with no emotion in her face. His eyes teared up as he said his final words, “Why sister? I loved you.” To which Tiamat replied, “Your love isn’t the one I’m after.”


After committing her crime Tiamat scattered Bahamut’s scales across the floor and in Sardior’s blood.


The next morning Tiamat ran to her father, fake tears in her eyes and said, “It’s Sardior father, he’s dead. Bahamut killed him!” Io stirred immediately and ran to Sardiors side to grieve his firstborn. Io was no fool however, as he examined the body he noticed five equally spaced bite marks along his son's back. He turned to Tiamat and told her to fetch her brother, which she happily did. When Bahamut arrived at the scene he wept and asked, “Who did this?” Tiamat was grinning but before she could say anything Io said “Your sister, Tiamat.” Before Tiamat could defend herself Io banished her from his realm.


Tiamat, now alone, decided it was no longer her purpose to serve Oerth as her father wished, no, instead she would destroy it. With that she changed her message to her children, “Destroy and conquer,” she said, “This world is now yours”


This sparked a ten-thousand year long war between Tiamat and her chromatic dragons and the combined forces of Bahamut, his dragons, and the remaining gem dragons.


In this war the Dragonborn and Kobolds arose, created by dragons to be their foot soldiers; they had the distinct ancestry of whatever dragon lineage they were created from.


After 10,000 years Bahamut finally found and fought his sister, they were evenly matched. It became clear to them that if they continued fighting they would both die, an option they were both happy with.


Upon seeing this Io made a decision. After watching one of his children and thousands of dragonkind die he couldn’t allow this slaughter to continue anymore. 


He appeared in front of Bahamut and Tiamat and said, “If blood need be shed, let that which is shed be mine!” and with that he sliced open his own belly, his blood pooled around Tiamat’s feet and then slowly turned into chains that wrapped around her. She fought as hard as she could but was no match. Io’s blood chained her and dragged her down into the first of the nine hells, Avernus, where she remains to this day


With that the war ended, the remaining dragons and dragonborn dispersed across the world. Bahamut stayed as a protector, doing his best to keep back all in the world that is evil, awaiting the return of his accursed sister. 
  

# Dragonborn in the World Today

While dragonborn are free beings, not bound by the alignment of their dragon forebears, many dragonborn clans hold intense prejudices against each other that stem back from the Dragon War. Many metallic dragonborn see chromatic dragonborn as lesser beings waiting for any chance to assist in bringing Tiamat back to destroy the world.

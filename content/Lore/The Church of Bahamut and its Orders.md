# Orders and Hierarchy

While anyone is welcome to join the church and attend services, only dragonborn may be a member of the clergy

The Church of Bahamut is made of several sects called “orders”. While there is no definite centralization these groups all work together to add some structure.

No church in a town is ever made up of just one order, paladins, priests and clerics of several orders will work together in one church.

Note that a dragonborns scale color has nothing to do with which order they can join: a gold dragonborn can be in the Order of Copper just as a copper dragonborn can be in the Order of Gold. In fact even other races may join orders, however they are barred from the Order of Platinum due to its restrictions.

Similarly, joining an order is optional. While most members of the clergy are in one it is not a requirement.


### Order of Platinum/Ruby

- Reserved for the highest ranking members of the other orders.
- Acts as a high council of sorts
- Never more than 20 members at a time (3 from each order (except Crystal and Topaz) : one Archpriest, one Grandmaster Paladin, and one Archcleric) and 5 Champions. 
- A metallic dragonborn that has gone through the process of becoming an Archpriest, Grandmaster Paladin, and Archcleric may become a Champion of Bahamut
- A gem dragonborn that has gone through the process of becoming an Archpriest, Grandmaster Paladin, and Archcleric may become a Champion of Sardior


### Order of Gold/Amethyst

- The Order of Gold primarily associates itself with preserving order and justice, primarily through quests
- The “Swords” of Bahamut

### Order of Silver/Sapphire

- The Order of Silver primarily associates itself with protecting cities, towns, and villages from external threats
- The “Shields” of Bahamut

### Order of Bronze

- The Order of Bronze primarily associates itself with public works and preservation of knowledge
- Note the knowledge the Order of Bronze preserves is that of engineering and the sciences, knowledge of history and culture is preserved by the Order of Brass

### Order of Brass/Emerald

- The Order of Brass primarily associates itself with preserving history, lore, and culture

### Order of Copper

- The Order of Copper primarily associates itself with the arts and artisanry
- Nearly all armors, weapons, clothes, and tools used by the church are made by a member of the Order of Copper.
- Usually a given church in a city, town, or village will have at least 1 or 2 members of the Order of Copper guaranteed

### Order of Crystal

- Doctors and caregivers
- Mostly non clergy and civilian

### Order of Topaz

- Perform funeral rites
- Keep to themselves
- Mostly non clergy and civilian

# Church Operations and Belief System

The church does several unorthodox things that are quite strange compared to other religions on Oerth.

- They pay their taxes
	- While no church or temple is required to pay tax in most cities, members of the Church of Bahamut insist upon paying them.
	- They do this partially to shun the massive hoards of gold and trinkets that chromatic dragons gather. They don’t want to hoard wealth like the evil dragons do.
	- They also do this as a practical part of their belief system. Their only true goal is that of the original dragons created by Bahamut, to spread good in the world and protect it. While they admit a portion of the taxes they pay may go to some bad people, they also see them as necessary for the cities and villages they want to protect to function.
- It’s been mentioned above but it’s worth putting here that the church lacks any real centralization. It has structures and rules but they do not answer to any one authority.
- Despite the name of their church, members of the church do not worship Bahamut, they simply follow his original order given to the original metallic dragons: spread good into the world and protect it. This is why they lack centralization: their beliefs aren’t based on worship, they’re based on doing good.
- They do not follow their religion in promise of a “heaven” or to escape a “hell” they do it solely to make the world a better place
